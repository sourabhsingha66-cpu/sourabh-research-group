About

I am a PhD student at IIT Bombay (Chemical Engineering / Materials Modeling) focused on atomistic simulations of hydrogen in metals. My research includes layer-by-layer hydride formation, strain-driven diffusion at α/β interfaces, and developing ML surrogates for diffusion barriers. This site combines my research profile with collaborator summaries and project links.

Research Interests

  • Hydrogen storage in metals (Pd–H, Ni–H)
  • Layer-by-layer hydride formation and nucleation
  • Strain effects on diffusion and interface energetics
  • Molecular Dynamics (GROMACS / LAMMPS) & NEB calculations
  • Kinetic Monte Carlo models for macroscopic kinetics
  • Machine learning for predicting diffusion barriers

Selected Projects

PCT diagram generation (EAM)

Constructed P–C–T diagrams using EAM potentials; performed VDoS analysis from VACF and statistical post-processing.

PdH layer-by-layer modelling

Atomistic simulations investigating layer growth, composition evolution and strain coupling across (1-x)Pd + xPdH → PdHx.

Diffusion barrier ML model

Training surrogate models on a large dataset (~1M barriers) to accelerate barrier estimation for KMC inputs.

GROMACS: solvated Li‑polysulfide

MD simulations of solvated lithium polysulfides; force field setup and analysis scripts available on request.
