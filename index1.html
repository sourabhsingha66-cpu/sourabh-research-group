About the group

We investigate chemo-mechanical and atomistic processes in metal hydrides and related materials with a focus on hydrogen storage — combining molecular dynamics (MD), kinetic Monte Carlo (KMC), first-principles-informed interatomic potentials (EAM), and machine learning to build fast predictive models of diffusion barriers and phase transformation behavior.

Core competencies

Molecular Dynamics (GROMACS, custom LAMMPS workflows) Kinetic Monte Carlo Interatomic potentials (EAM) DFT-informed inputs Machine learning for diffusion barriers (GNNs, SchNet/DimeNet) PCT diagrams & thermodynamics

Research highlights

  • Layer-by-layer hydride formation in metal hydrides — atomistic models to capture α/β interface formation and strain-coupled diffusion barriers.
  • Pd ↔ PdH composition studies: (1-x)Pd + xPdH → PdHx, phase behaviour and kinetics at elevated temperatures (e.g. 500 K studies).
  • Large-scale dataset of diffusion barriers (~1 million entries) used to train ML surrogates that reduce prediction time from days to minutes.
  • PCT diagram construction using EAM potentials and vibrational density of states (VDOS) to compute thermodynamic properties.

Tools & files we produce

  • Unit cell generators (.xyz, .cif) for BCC/FCC/hcp systems.
  • Simulation input suites: GROMACS topologies, LAMMPS scripts, Fortran/Octave utilities for common-tangent and PCT processing.
  • Analysis code — VACF → VDOS pipelines, convergence checks, and plotting scripts.

How we work

We combine computational modeling (MD, DFT data) and ML model-building. Our pipelines prioritize reproducibility: documented input files, unit-cell generators, provably converged VACF/VDOS workflows, and a modular ML training pipeline for GNNs and descriptor-based regressors.

Selected projects
